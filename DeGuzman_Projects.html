This is my Entreprenuership Project. For this project our objective was to think about a problem in the world today and come up with a solution to it whether it would be computerized, mechanically, or both. My group and I created the LifeSensor, which is a system integrated into a car that detects to see if you left your pet or baby in the car after you parked. Basically what it does is, after you park it will first check to see if it dark outside. If it were to be dark outside it would leave the car how it is. However, if it were sunny or hot outside, the system will go through its safety protocols. These safety protocols include ultrasonic sensors which will check to see if there is any movement in the car. Had it detected any movement, it will turn on an alarm, and an emergency cooling system that doesn't require the power of the car. In our car prototype, the flashlight was used to simulate the alarm going off, while the fan was to simulate an emergency cooling system. As you can see in the video attached to the link below, the car does not turn on the fan and flashlight, when the light sensor is covered (simulating darkness). For this project, I was the group manager, where I coded the program, helped construct the prototype, and finally assist my group to make sure that we were going to stay on task to complete the project by deadline. I applied a lot of engineering concepts in this particular project. First, I was able to use my RobotC program language taught by my teacher Ms. Chou to program the code needed for the safety system. Next, I was able to work with the VEX programmable parts and integrate them into the prototype car such as light sensors, ultrasonic sensors, buttons, and more. Finally, I applied my previous knowledge from my previous projects to help construct our final prototype. I feel that this project was the ultimate culmination of everything that I had learned in POE. It was such a great feeling to merge the 2 different fields of engineering. I was able to use the regular engineering side of the academy to construct the prototype, and use the computer engineering side to code the program needed for the safety protocols. From group manager's perspective, I took the next step into leading a team once more, and feel that I did all that I could to put my team in a position to compete for a spot in the showcase. Although, we were not able to make it to the showcase, I know that we worked hard and did the best we could to solve a growing problem today. The LifeSensor, given the proper technology and expertise, could be elevated to a better product for the market. With improvements such as an actual creation of the emergency cooling system and alarm, integration of infrared sensors, and better interior sensors other than ultrasonics, the LifeSensor has the capability to be licensed to other car companies already existing in the market.
